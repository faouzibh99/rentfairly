Why You Need a Roommate Agreement

A lease agreement protects the landlord. A roommate agreement protects you and your roommates from each other. Without one, you're relying entirely on verbal promises — which are almost impossible to enforce and easy to misremember.

Key things a roommate agreement covers: who pays what amount of rent, how utilities are split, rules about guests and noise, what happens if someone wants to leave early, and how shared spaces are managed.

Tips for a Successful Roommate Agreement

  • Be specific — "clean up after yourself" is useless. "Dishes must be washed within 24 hours" is enforceable.
  • Include everything — no detail is too small if it could cause conflict later.
  • All roommates must sign — a one-sided agreement isn't worth the paper it's on.
  • Keep a copy each — everyone should have a signed copy in their email or photo album.
  • Review annually — circumstances change. Revisit the agreement once a year.
