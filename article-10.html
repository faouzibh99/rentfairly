The Fairest Way to Choose Rooms When Moving In Together

Choosing who gets which room is one of the first potential conflicts when moving in together. Here are the fairest ways to settle it — before anyone gets attached.

Why Room Selection Matters

The room you choose determines your rent — especially with a fair rent split calculator. Choosing rooms is not just about preference; it is a financial decision. Getting it right from the start prevents almost all room-related resentment.

Method 1: The Bidding System (Most Fair)

Each roommate privately submits how much they are willing to pay for each room. The person who bids highest for each room gets it and pays that amount. The total must equal the full rent.

Why it works: Rooms go to the people who value them most. No one is forced into a room at a price they think is unfair.

Method 2: Fair Calculator + Random Draw (Recommended)

Use a fair rent calculator to set objective prices for each room based on size and features. Then draw names randomly to assign rooms. Everyone knows the price before the draw — no surprises.

This is the method we recommend for most roommates: transparent, objective, and fair.

Method 3: First Come, First Served

Whoever calls a room first gets it. The most common method and the least fair. It rewards speed over preference and ignores room value entirely. Only works when all rooms are genuinely similar.

Method 4: Draft Order

Randomly determine a pick order, then each person picks their room in turn. Reverse the order for the second round if there are more items to allocate.

Method 5: Coin Flip

For two roommates with two similar rooms, a coin flip is perfectly legitimate. Both agree the rooms are roughly equal, and chance decides. Simple and fast.

The one rule that applies to every method: agree on the process BEFORE you see the apartment. Once people have formed preferences, any method feels rigged to whoever does not get their first choice.
