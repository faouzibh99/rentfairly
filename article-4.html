10 Roommate Money Rules Every House Should Have

Money is the #1 cause of roommate conflicts. These 10 rules, agreed on before you move in, prevent almost all of them.

Rule 1: Agree on Rent Splits Before Moving In

Never move in without a written agreement on who pays what. Use a fair calculator, not an equal split assumption. Get it in writing.

Rule 2: Set One Payment Deadline

Pick one date and stick to it. Everyone pays by that date, no exceptions and no reminders needed.

Rule 3: Use a Shared Expense App

Apps like Splitwise track who paid what so no one has to keep score mentally. This alone eliminates most money arguments between roommates.

Rule 4: Never Float Money for a Roommate Repeatedly

Covering a roommate once is generous. Doing it regularly creates financial resentment and an unhealthy dynamic. Be firm about this early.

Rule 5: Separate Shared Groceries from Personal Food

Either share all food with equal contributions, or keep separate shelves. The unclear middle ground causes endless friction.

Rule 6: Create a Shared Household Fund

Each roommate contributes $20-30/month to a shared fund for toilet paper, cleaning supplies, and household items. No one tracks who bought the dish soap.

Rule 7: Discuss Big Shared Purchases Together

Before buying a shared TV or appliance, agree in writing on who owns it if someone moves out.

Rule 8: Handle Late Payments Immediately

If a roommate is late on rent, address it immediately. A kind, direct conversation at day 3 is far easier than a conflict at month 3.

Rule 9: The Shared Fund Is Not a Personal Loan

The household fund is for household expenses only — not a personal loan fund for any roommate, ever.

Rule 10: Revisit the Agreement Every Year

Incomes change, situations change, people change. Schedule a 30-minute house meeting once a year to review the financial arrangement.
